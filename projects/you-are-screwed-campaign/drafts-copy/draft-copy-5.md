# "Følg Din Krone" — Landing page copy v5

_Structural shift from v4: Conversion-first → Storytelling-first. The page is no longer a funnel. It's a narrative the reader walks through. The score is no longer the hook — it's the mirror you encounter after you understand the system. The protagonist is the street, not the individual. The tone is a smart friend explaining something over dinner, not a TED talk. The title changes from confrontational provocation to quiet curiosity._

_Key changes from v4:_

- _Replaced "How Screwed Are You?" with "Følg Din Krone" (Follow Your Krone) — curiosity-led, not confrontational_
- _Restructured from conversion funnel to five-act narrative arc_
- _Moved the score calculator from hero to mid-page (earned, not given)_
- _Bill breakdown becomes a scroll-triggered progressive revelation (the centerpiece)_
- _FAQ eliminated as a section — honesty is woven into the story_
- _Final CTA shifts from "check my score" to "start the conversation on your street"_
- _Added three new narrative elements: The Krone's Journey, Your Street Reimagined, The Money Loop_
- _Alternative titles proposed at end_

---

## Narrative overview

**The story in one sentence:** Your street is the fourth cooperative — dairy, wind, housing, and now electricity.

**The arc:** You pay a bill every month without thinking about it → you follow a single krone through six layers of infrastructure you never chose → you discover Danes solved this exact problem three times before → you see what your street looks like when it owns the infrastructure → you realize you're not alone in this → you talk to your neighbor.

**The protagonist:** Not you. Your street. A collection of households that share the same tariffs, the same grid company, the same silence about it. The character arc: isolation → recognition → cooperation.

**The villain:** Not greed. Distance. The physical and structural distance between you and your electricity — kilometers of cable, layers of intermediaries, fees that exist to pay for the journey. The system isn't evil. It's just long.

**The tone:** Dinner-party conversation. A friend who happens to know a lot about energy policy, explaining something genuinely interesting. Never heated. Never salesy. Dry, understated, occasionally funny. Read every sentence aloud — if it sounds like a Facebook ad from a mattress company, cut it. If it sounds like something you'd actually say while cooking, keep it.

**The emotional arc:**

| Section             | Reader feels           | Narrative function                      |
| ------------------- | ---------------------- | --------------------------------------- |
| Hero                | Mild curiosity         | Ordinary world — plant a question       |
| The Krone's Journey | Accumulating disbelief | Revelation — watch the money disappear  |
| The History         | Quiet recognition      | Pattern — you've seen this before       |
| The Score           | Sharp clarity          | Mirror — now it's personal              |
| Your Street         | Concrete imagination   | Vision — this is what it looks like     |
| The Honest Part     | Grounded realism       | Trust — we're not hiding the hard parts |
| The Close           | Quiet readiness        | Invitation — talk to your neighbor      |

## [Act 2: The Revelation]

### The Krone's Journey — a scroll-triggered progressive revelation

_This is the centerpiece. The bill breakdown bar from v4, reimagined as a narrative experience. As the reader scrolls, each fee layer builds up one at a time, eating into the krone. The reader watches their money disappear in real time. This section should feel like slowly unwrapping a mystery — not reading a chart._

_Design: Each layer slides in as the reader scrolls. Between layers, a short line of text contextualizes what they just saw. The green "electricity" segment is always visible as the shrinking remainder. By the end, the full bar is assembled — and the reader understands every piece._

**Section heading:**

### Følg din krone gennem systemet.

---

Her er én krone fra din elregning. Lad os se hvor den ender.

Nettarif. Det er hvad det koster at vedligeholde det net der fører strømmen til din stikkontakt. Et net du bruger hver dag. Et net du ikke ejer.

**Layer 2 — Elafgift (22 øre)**

Så 22 øre mere.

Elafgift. En afgift der engang finansierede den grønne omstilling. Det var en god idé. Afgiften eksisterer stadig. Formålet er lidt mere uklart.

**Layer 3 — Moms (20 øre)**

20 øre til moms. Selvfølgelig.

Du betaler moms på tarifferne. Du betaler moms på afgifterne. Du betaler moms på de gebyrer der betaler for infrastrukturen der leverer den el du endnu ikke har nået til.

_[Bar: 70% claimed. 30% remaining.]_

**Layer 4 — Systemtarif (8 øre)**

8 øre til systemtarif.

Det er prisen for at balancere elnettet — sørge for at udbud og efterspørgsel matcher sekund for sekund. Et vigtigt job. Et job du ingen indflydelse har på.

**Layer 5 — Transmissionstarif (6 øre)**

6 øre til transmissionstarif.

Det er prisen for at transportere el hundredevis af kilometer fra kraftværk til din by. Afstanden koster. Hvert lag af infrastruktur undervejs tager sin bid.

Og så er der det der er tilbage.

16 øre. Det er din faktiske el. Den strøm der tænder lampen, kører køleskabet, lader telefonen. Alt det du troede du betalte for.

_[The full bar is now visible — all six layers. A moment of stillness before the text appears.]_

Elektricitet produceres langt væk. Den rejser gennem dyr infrastruktur. Den passerer regulerede instanser der hver tager deres andel. Den ankommer til din måler med hvert lags omkostninger bagt ind.

Du modtager. Du betaler. Pengene forlader din gade og kommer ikke tilbage.

**Ingen designede det her for at snyde dig.** Det var bare aldrig designet til dig. Det var designet til centraliserede kraftværker og envejs-levering. Og det virkede — det elektrificierede Danmark. Men det var en anden tid.

## [Act 3: The Pattern]

### Tre gange har danskere løst det her problem.

_This section is the narrative center of gravity. It tells the 150-year story of Danish cooperation — not as bullet points, but as a story. The tone is almost campfire-like. "Let me tell you what happened."_

---

**Første gang: Mælken**

I 1880'erne solgte danske bønder deres mælk til private mejerier. Mejerierne satte priserne. Bønderne havde ingen indflydelse. Mælken var deres, men infrastrukturen var en andens.

Så de byggede deres egne. Andelsmejerier. Ejet af bønderne selv. Beslutninger taget på generalforsamlinger. Overskud delt efter hvor meget mælk du leverede, ikke hvor mange penge du havde. Inden for tyve år var det den dominerende model i dansk landbrug.

Mekanismen: **Hvorfor betale en anden for at eje det vi alle er afhængige af?**

---

**Anden gang: Vinden**

I 1970'erne og 80'erne begyndte grupper af danskere at opstille vindmøller sammen. Vindmøllelaug. Du investerede med dine naboer, du delte strømmen, du ejede møllen. Ikke en startup. Ikke et firma. En forening.

Det var den model der gjorde Danmark til verdensleder inden for vindenergi. Ikke en statslig strategi oppefra. Almindelige mennesker der slog sig sammen.

Mekanismen: **Den samme.**

---

**Tredje gang: Boligerne**

Halvdelen af København bor i andelsboliger. Du ejer ikke din lejlighed. Foreningen ejer bygningen. Du ejer din andel af foreningen. Generalforsamlinger. Kollektive beslutninger. Fælles ansvar.

Det er ikke utopi. Det er hverdag. Enhver der har siddet til en andelsboligforenings generalforsamling kl. 21 en tirsdag aften ved det er hverdag.

Mekanismen: **Stadig den samme.**

---

**Den fjerde gang venter.**

Dine forfædre kiggede på mælken og sagde: vi kan eje mejeriet. Deres børn kiggede på vinden og sagde: vi kan eje møllen. Deres børnebørn kiggede på boligerne og sagde: vi kan eje bygningen.

Din elregning er det samme slags problem. Infrastruktur du er afhængig af, ejet af nogen der ikke er dig. Penge der forlader din gade.

Det hedder et **Energifællesskab**. En juridisk anerkendt kooperativ struktur — defineret under EU-lovgivning, støttet af dansk regulering, bakket op af dedikerede finansieringsprogrammer.

Og spørgsmålet det besvarer er ikke "hvilken udbyder er billigst?"

**Det er: "Hvorfor har du brug for en udbyder overhovedet?"**

---

_Design note: This section should breathe. Generous white space between the three cooperative stories. Each one lands, then the next begins. The typography might shift — slightly larger for the "mechanism" lines, creating a visual rhythm. By the time the reader reaches "Den fjerde gang venter," they should feel the pattern in their bones._

## [Act 4.5: The Honest Part]

### Hvad det faktisk kræver.

_No separate FAQ section. No accordion. Just honest, open text that acknowledges what's hard. This section builds trust precisely because it doesn't oversell._

---

**Opening:**

Vi kunne stoppe her og lade det lyde nemt. Det ville bare ikke være sandt. Så her er den ærlige version.

---

**Om tid:**

At danne et andelsselskab kræver arbejde. Generalforsamlinger, kollektive beslutninger, papirarbejde. Det er ikke en et-klik-løsning. De fleste fællesskaber er 6–12 måneder om at blive sat op. Det vi kan gøre er at håndtere den tekniske og reguleringsmæssige kompleksitet. Den del kun I kan gøre er at tale med jeres naboer.

---

**Om penge:**

Et Energifællesskab har reelle startomkostninger — udstyr, juridisk struktur, nettilslutning. EU og den danske stat har øremærket midler specifikt til borger-energiinitiativer. De er designet til at dække størstedelen, men "typisk dækket" er ikke "altid dækket." Vi viser dig præcis hvilke programmer dit postnummer kvalificerer til, og hvad de realistiske udgifter ser ud.

---

**Om lejere:**

Energifællesskaber er bygget på andelsprincippet: alle der deltager, har gavn af det. Du behøver ikke eje et tag. De reguleringsmæssige detaljer for lejerdeltagelse i Danmark er stadig under udarbejdelse — men EU-rammen er eksplicit designet til at inkludere dig. Vi holder dig opdateret når reglerne falder på plads.

---

**Om skepsis:**

Det er ikke magi. Det er infrastruktur plus andelslovgivning plus kollektiv handling. Matematikken holder fordi I fjerner strukturelle lag, ikke fordi nogen subsidierer jer. Danmark har gjort det her med mejerier, boliger og energi i 150 år. Den eneste nye del er at anvende det på din elregning.

Grunden til at flere ikke har gjort det endnu? De fleste ved ikke det er en mulighed. Ikke fordi det ikke virker.

---

_Design note: No collapsible sections. Everything is visible. The act of showing it all — including the hard parts — IS the trust mechanism. Danish readers will respect this more than a polished FAQ that sounds like objection-handling._

## Alternative titles

_"How Screwed Are You?" is punchy but conversion-brained. For a storytelling page, the title should invite curiosity, not provocation. Here are alternatives:_

**1. "Følg Din Krone" (Follow Your Krone)** — _Recommended._ Curiosity-led. Invites a journey. Works as both a literal description (follow the money) and a metaphor (follow the story). Neutral enough for Danish sensibilities. Sets up the scroll experience.

**2. "16 Øre"** — Minimalist. The number is the hook. Forces the reader to ask "16 øre of what?" Ultra-Danish in its understatement.

**3. "Din Gades Regning" (Your Street's Bill)** — Collective framing from the start. Positions the story as about the neighborhood, not the individual.

**4. "Hvor Ender Pengene?" (Where Does the Money Go?)** — Question format. Curiosity without confrontation. Universal — everyone has asked this about something.

**5. "Den Fjerde Gang" (The Fourth Time)** — Narrative. Only makes sense after reading the page — which is the point. Creates a mystery. Requires the cooperative history section to land.

**6. "Fra Din Konto Til —" (From Your Account To —)** — Incomplete sentence. The dash creates a gap the reader wants to fill. Sets up the krone's journey metaphor.

_For A/B testing, the most interesting matchup would be "Følg Din Krone" (curiosity) vs. "16 Øre" (minimalism) vs. "How Screwed Are You?" (provocation). Each targets a different psychological entry point._

## Variants and testing ideas

### Storytelling vs. Conversion A/B test

The most important test: run v4 (conversion) against v5 (storytelling) and compare:

- **Scroll depth** — Do storytelling readers get further?
- **Time on page** — Do they spend more time?
- **Score completion rate** — Does delaying the form reduce or increase completions?
- **Share rate** — Does the narrative framing produce more shares?
- **Share quality** — Do shared links convert better? (Storytelling may produce more personal shares, conversion may produce more impulse shares)
- **Return visits** — Do storytelling readers come back?
- **Neighbor referrals** — Not just shares, but people from the same postcode checking their score after a friend did

### Score placement test

- **v4 position** (hero, immediate): Tests whether instant gratification drives more completions
- **v5 position** (mid-page, after revelation): Tests whether earned understanding drives deeper engagement
- **Hypothesis**: v4 gets more raw score checks. v5 gets fewer score checks but more shares and more neighborhood-level actions.

### The Krone's Journey pacing test

- **Free scroll**: Reader controls pace, layers appear as they scroll past trigger points
- **Scroll-locked**: Reader's scroll is "captured" during the revelation — each layer appears on a timer, reader watches passively for 45 seconds
- **Hypothesis**: Scroll-locking is more dramatic but may frustrate impatient users. Free scroll respects autonomy (more Danish). Test on mobile vs. desktop separately.

### Title test

- "Følg Din Krone" vs. "16 Øre" vs. "How Screwed Are You?"
- Measure: click-through rate from social shares, bounce rate on landing, scroll depth
- Hypothesis: "How Screwed Are You?" gets the highest click-through but the highest bounce. "Følg Din Krone" gets moderate clicks but deepest engagement. "16 Øre" polarizes — very high engagement from those who click, but lower click-through.

## Production notes

### What needs to be real before launch

- **Testimonials**: Still needed. V4's placeholder policy stands: real quotes from real people only. The storytelling version needs them less urgently (the narrative carries more weight) but they still matter for the social proof section. Consider placing 1-2 real quotes inline within the "honest part" section rather than in a dedicated testimonial block.

- **Statistics**: Same rule — only publish verified numbers. If 47 people have checked their score, say 47. The storytelling version's credibility depends on honesty, not scale.

- **The "about" section**: Critical. Who is Orklys? What's the business model? Real names and faces. This cannot launch with placeholders.

- **Funding specifics**: The copy references EU and national funding programs. Before launch, replace "[PLACEHOLDER]" with actual program names, amounts, and qualification criteria by postcode.
