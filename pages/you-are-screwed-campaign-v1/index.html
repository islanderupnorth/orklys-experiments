A story about your electricity billEn fortælling om din elregning

Follow your krone,-Følg din krone,-

You pay an electricity bill every month. Everyone does.
But have you ever followed the money?Du betaler en elregning hver måned. Det gør alle.
Men har du nogensinde fulgt pengene?

Not just glanced at the total — but traced a single krone from your account, through the system, all the way to the other end?Ikke bare set tallet — men fulgt en enkelt krone fra din konto, gennem systemet, helt ud til den anden ende?

How much of your electricity bill is actually electricity?Hvor meget af din elregning tror du er faktisk el?

The average is 16–22 øre per krone.Gennemsnittet er 16–22 øre per krone.

The rest is tariffs, fees, and charges for infrastructure you've never seen.Resten er tariffer, gebyrer og afgifter for infrastruktur du aldrig har set.

Follow your krone through the system.Følg din krone gennem systemet.

Here's one krone from your electricity bill. Let's see where it ends up.Her er én krone fra din elregning. Lad os se hvor den ender.

Grid TariffNettarif

First, 28 øre disappear. That's what it costs to maintain the grid that carries electricity to your outlet. A grid you use every day. A grid you don't own.Først forsvinder 28 øre. Det er hvad det koster at vedligeholde det net der fører strømmen til din stikkontakt. Et net du bruger hver dag. Et net du ikke ejer.

22 øre

Electricity TaxElafgift

Then another 22 øre. A tax that once funded the green transition. That was a good idea. The tax is still here. The purpose has gotten a bit hazy.Så 22 øre mere. En afgift der engang finansierede den grønne omstilling. Det var en god idé. Afgiften eksisterer stadig. Formålet er lidt mere uklart.

20 øre

VATMoms

20 øre to VAT. Of course. You pay VAT on the tariffs. You pay VAT on the taxes. You pay VAT on the fees that pay for the infrastructure that delivers the electricity you haven't even gotten to yet.20 øre til moms. Selvfølgelig. Du betaler moms på tarifferne. Du betaler moms på afgifterne. Du betaler moms på de gebyrer der betaler for infrastrukturen der leverer den el du endnu ikke har nået til.

8 øre

System TariffSystemtarif

8 øre to the system tariff. The price of balancing the grid — making sure supply and demand match, second by second. An important job. A job you have zero say in.8 øre til systemtarif. Prisen for at balancere elnettet — sørge for at udbud og efterspørgsel matcher sekund for sekund. Et vigtigt job. Et job du ingen indflydelse har på.

6 øre

Transmission TariffTransmissionstarif

6 øre to the transmission tariff. The price of moving electricity hundreds of kilometres from the power plant to your town. Distance costs. Every layer of infrastructure along the way takes its cut.6 øre til transmissionstarif. Prisen for at transportere el hundredevis af kilometer fra kraftværk til din by. Afstanden koster. Hvert lag af infrastruktur undervejs tager sin bid.

16 øre

Actual ElectricityFaktisk el

And then there's what's left. 16 øre. That's your actual electricity. The current that lights the lamp, runs the fridge, charges the phone. Everything you thought you were paying for.Og så er der det der er tilbage. 16 øre. Det er din faktiske el. Den strøm der tænder lampen, kører køleskabet, lader telefonen. Alt det du troede du betalte for.

This isn't unusual. This is how the system is built.Det her er ikke unormalt. Det er sådan systemet er bygget.

Electricity is produced far away. It travels through expensive infrastructure. It passes through regulated entities that each take their share. It arrives at your meter with every layer's cost baked in.Elektricitet produceres langt væk. Den rejser gennem dyr infrastruktur. Den passerer regulerede instanser der hver tager deres andel. Den ankommer til din måler med hvert lags omkostninger bagt ind.

You receive. You pay. The money leaves your street and doesn't come back.Du modtager. Du betaler. Pengene forlader din gade og kommer ikke tilbage.

Nobody designed this to cheat you. It just was never designed for you.Ingen designede det her for at snyde dig. Det var bare aldrig designet til dig.

But this is Denmark.Men det her er Danmark.

And the Danes who came before you faced exactly this kind of problem. They just solved it for milk, wind, and housing instead of electricity.Og danskerne der kom før dig stod med præcis den her slags problem. De løste det bare for mælk, vind og boliger i stedet for el.

Three times, Danes have solved this exact problem.Tre gange har danskere løst det her problem.

1 1880s1880'erne

The MilkMælken

In the 1880s, Danish farmers sold their milk to private dairies. The dairies set the prices. The farmers had no say. The milk was theirs, but the infrastructure belonged to someone else.I 1880'erne solgte danske bønder deres mælk til private mejerier. Mejerierne satte priserne. Bønderne havde ingen indflydelse. Mælken var deres, men infrastrukturen var en andens.

So they built their own. Cooperative dairies. Owned by the farmers themselves. Decisions made at general assemblies. Profits shared according to how much milk you delivered, not how much money you had.Så de byggede deres egne. Andelsmejerier. Ejet af bønderne selv. Beslutninger taget på generalforsamlinger. Overskud delt efter hvor meget mælk du leverede, ikke hvor mange penge du havde.

The mechanism: Why pay someone else to own what we all depend on?Mekanismen: Hvorfor betale en anden for at eje det vi alle er afhængige af?

2 1970s1970'erne

The WindVinden

In the 1970s and 80s, groups of Danes started putting up wind turbines together. Wind cooperatives. You invested with your neighbors, you shared the power, you owned the turbine. Not a startup. Not a company. An association.I 1970'erne og 80'erne begyndte grupper af danskere at opstille vindmøller sammen. Vindmøllelaug. Du investerede med dine naboer, du delte strømmen, du ejede møllen. Ikke en startup. Ikke et firma. En forening.

That model made Denmark the world leader in wind energy. Not a top-down government strategy. Ordinary people pooling resources.Det var den model der gjorde Danmark til verdensleder inden for vindenergi. Ikke en statslig strategi oppefra. Almindelige mennesker der slog sig sammen.

The mechanism: The same one.Mekanismen: Den samme.

3 TodayI dag

HousingBoligerne

Half of Copenhagen lives in cooperative housing. You don't own your apartment. The association owns the building. You own your share of the association. General assemblies. Collective decisions. Shared responsibility.Halvdelen af København bor i andelsboliger. Du ejer ikke din lejlighed. Foreningen ejer bygningen. Du ejer din andel af foreningen. Generalforsamlinger. Kollektive beslutninger. Fælles ansvar.

It's not utopia. It's a Tuesday. Anyone who has sat through a housing cooperative's general assembly at 9 PM on a weeknight knows it's very much a Tuesday.Det er ikke utopi. Det er hverdag. Enhver der har siddet til en andelsboligforenings generalforsamling kl. 21 en tirsdag aften ved det er hverdag.

The mechanism: Still the same.Mekanismen: Stadig den samme.

The fourth time is waiting.Den fjerde gang venter.

Your electricity bill is the same kind of problem. Infrastructure you depend on, owned by someone who isn't you. Money leaving your street.Din elregning er det samme slags problem. Infrastruktur du er afhængig af, ejet af nogen der ikke er dig. Penge der forlader din gade.

It's called an Energy Community. A legally recognized cooperative structure — defined under EU legislation, supported by Danish regulation, backed by dedicated funding programs.Det hedder et Energifællesskab. En juridisk anerkendt kooperativ struktur — defineret under EU-lovgivning, støttet af dansk regulering, bakket op af dedikerede finansieringsprogrammer.

And the question it answers is not "which provider is cheapest?"
It is: "Why do you need a provider at all?"Og spørgsmålet det besvarer er ikke "hvilken udbyder er billigst?"
Det er: "Hvorfor har du brug for en udbyder overhovedet?"

We could stop here and let it sound easy. It just wouldn't be true. So here's the honest version.Vi kunne stoppe her og lade det lyde nemt. Det ville bare ikke være sandt. Så her er den ærlige version.

About timeOm tid

Setting up a cooperative takes work. General meetings, collective decisions, paperwork. This is not a one-click solution. Most communities take 6 to 12 months to get established. What we can do is handle the technical and regulatory complexity. The part only you can do is talk to your neighbors.At danne et andelsselskab kræver arbejde. Generalforsamlinger, kollektive beslutninger, papirarbejde. Det er ikke en et-klik-løsning. De fleste fællesskaber er 6–12 måneder om at blive sat op. Det vi kan gøre er at håndtere den tekniske og reguleringsmæssige kompleksitet. Den del kun I kan gøre er at tale med jeres naboer.

About moneyOm penge

An Energy Community has real startup costs — equipment, legal structure, grid connection. The EU and the Danish state have earmarked funding specifically for citizen energy initiatives. The programs are designed to cover the majority, but "typically covered" is not "always covered." We show you exactly which programs your postcode qualifies for, and what the realistic costs look like.Et Energifællesskab har reelle startomkostninger — udstyr, juridisk struktur, nettilslutning. EU og den danske stat har øremærket midler specifikt til borger-energiinitiativer. De er designet til at dække størstedelen, men "typisk dækket" er ikke "altid dækket." Vi viser dig præcis hvilke programmer dit postnummer kvalificerer til, og hvad de realistiske udgifter ser ud.

About rentersOm lejere

Energy Communities are built on the cooperative principle: everyone who participates, benefits. You don't need to own a roof. The regulatory details for renter participation in Denmark are still being finalized — but the EU framework is explicitly designed to include you.Energifællesskaber er bygget på andelsprincippet: alle der deltager, har gavn af det. Du behøver ikke eje et tag. De reguleringsmæssige detaljer for lejerdeltagelse i Danmark er stadig under udarbejdelse — men EU-rammen er eksplicit designet til at inkludere dig.

About skepticismOm skepsis

It's not magic. It's infrastructure plus cooperative law plus collective action. The math works because you remove structural layers, not because someone subsidizes you. Denmark has done this with dairies, housing, and energy for 150 years. The only new part is applying it to your electricity bill.Det er ikke magi. Det er infrastruktur plus andelslovgivning plus kollektiv handling. Matematikken holder fordi I fjerner strukturelle lag, ikke fordi nogen subsidierer jer. Danmark har gjort det her med mejerier, boliger og energi i 150 år. Den eneste nye del er at anvende det på din elregning.

Why haven't more people done it? Most don't know it's an option. Not because it doesn't work.Grunden til at flere ikke har gjort det endnu? De fleste ved ikke det er en mulighed. Ikke fordi det ikke virker.
